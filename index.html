About me

Since September 2025, I am a Walter Noll Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Carnegie Mellon University.

Previously, I was a doctoral student (PhD) at the Max Planck Institut for Mathematics in the Sciences in Leipzig, Germany, under the supervision of Prof. Felix Otto.

My research interests are focussed on parabolic differential equation, especially mean curvature flow and the heat flow on general spaces. Furthermore, I am also interested at the intersection of analysis with geometry, numerics, probability theory and graph theory.

Research

In this section, I present some of my research projects and directions together with their applications.
All the image and videos which are not explicitely specified are created by myself. I used matlab, python, rust and unity (C♯ and HLSL).

  • Diffusion of Heat - Heat Flow

    A solution of the heat equation, the L2-gradient flow (GF) of the Dirichlet energy. It shows how heat distributes in an area.

  • Soap Bubbles - Volume preserving Mean Curvature Flow (VMCF)

    Soap bubbles minimize their surface area under the constrained that they contain a fixed volume of air. This is modelled by mean curvature flow which is formally the gradient flow of the surface area / surface tension. In this video, the simulation is done by a median filter scheme together with a ray marcher and implementation of the eikonal equation and heat flow.

  • Fluid Phase Separation - Allen-Cahn Equation

    Here, one can see the densities of two fluids (red and blue) which begin mixed but separate out over time. This is modelled by the Allen-Cahn equation which is also the L2-GF of the free Ginzburgh-Landau energy.

  • Hybrid Zones in Mice Genes - Allen-Cahn and SLFVPS

    The picture is from doi:10.1038/hdy.1973.56 . It shows the hybrid zones in the alleles of mice in denmark. The formation of these can be modelled by the Allen-Cahn equation and simulated for instance Spatial Lambda Fleming Viot Processes with Selection (SLFVPS)

  • Metal Properties - Multiphase MCF

    The local structure of metals dictates their material properties. For instance, if the metal has a lot of grains (grain boundaries are local dislocations in the orientation of the atoms), it will be very brittle. On the other hand, materials with few grains are very ductile. This is for instance used in cars, where the crumble zone gives in to pressure while the sides require more force and will break instead of bend. The experimental data is from link.

  • Water Droplets - VMCF Young Angles

    Water droplets on different surfaces have varying contact angles depending on whether the surface is hydrophilic or hydrophobic. For instance, on a lotus paddle, the contact angle is very high while on glass it is shallow. This can be modelled with Young angles and volume preserving mean curvature flow.

  • Visualization MCF

    The video shows a visualization of mean curvature flow in three dimensions with a two dimensional surface. Even though the initial data is smooth, singularities will develop in finite time.

  • Image Processing - Median filter

    A median filter can be used to remove noise from an image without changing the relevant features and edges. The original cat image is under a CC0 license.

  • Soap Film Wire - MCF Dirichlet Data

    If one lifts a metal wire out of a bath of soap, the remaining film will become a minimal surface. As these are the stationary solutions to MCF, one can model this behaviour with MCF with Dirichlet boundary conditions.

  • Unsupervised Learning MNIST Classification - Glued Manifolds, Multiphase MCF on Manifolds

    In unsupervised learning, the objective is to learn the structure of data without labeling. Here, one has 60.000 hand-drawn digits as 28x28 greyscale images. The task is a classification task, i.e., one wants to have a program that gives each image the right label. For the modelling of the data one can use the union of manifold hypothesis and model it as a glued manifold. The algorithm can be created by a (potentially volume preserving) multiphase mean curvature algorithm to minimize the area between the sets.

  • Two-Balloon Experiment - VMCF

    In this experiment, one has two connected balloons, one small, one large. Now the pressure inside these will average out and it turns out that the small balloon will shrink while the larger one grows. This can also be modelled and observed with volume preserving mean curvature flow.

  • Visualization of the MBO Scheme

    The MBO or Thresholding scheme is a very efficient scheme to simulate mean curvature flow. It was developed by Merriman-Bence-Osher and uses the reaction diffusion character of mean curvature flow. On the euclidean space this scheme can be implemented using the discrete fast Fourier transform.
